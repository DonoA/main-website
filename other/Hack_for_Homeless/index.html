The Background

Nowadays, almost everybody owns a cellular phone that can connect to the internet - including 70% of the homeless population in Santa Clara County. These phones can provide them access to resources they wouldn't have otherwise, such as information on food services, medical help, and other aid. They also serve as the sole means by which a homeless person can be contacted, as they don't have a static home address or home phone.


The Problem

Unfortunately, there are very few applications built specifically for the homeless population. This hackathon aims to change that. In just one day, you can create a smartphone app or website that could aid the homeless for years to come. We've listed some projects students worked on at last year's Hack for the Homeless for inspiration, but feel free to come up with your own genius ideas. So come out and join us, and make a difference in the lives of the Bay Area's homeless population.



OPPORTUNITY: Integration With CTA Phones

The Community Technology Alliance is offering the unique opportunity to have standout apps come preloaded on all phones in their Mobile For All campaign. These phones will be distributed to impoverished individuals in the US who would otherwise not have access to cellular services. This is a huge initiative that aims to increase the self-sufficiency of low income and homeless individuals, and the opportunity to get your app included in their phone program will really cement your accomplishments.


CASE STUDY: Finding Health Clinics

A team created an app that allowed the user to find a free or low-cost clinic that suited their needs. The app allowed the user to refine their search based on the following criteria: what population is served, location, hours, and services provided. The app interfaced with a database to search for clinics that met the criteria. Future enhancements to the app are set to include: Displaying results on a map relative to the user's current location, and showing the user the closest results by both distance and time on public transit.


CASE STUDY: Food Notifications

Last year, a team created an app that provided the user with information about free meals being offered near them, at given times. For example, if the nearest location serving lunch opened at 12:00, the user would receive a notification at 11:30 to notify them, along with the next two nearest lunch services. The app interfaced with a database to find organizations serving food. This app displayed the results on a map, relative to the user's current location. Future enhancements are set to include showing the user the closest results by both distance and time on public transit.

FAQ

Who can attend?

This hackathon is open to all college students who can make the trip! A ".edu" email account is required for registration.

When and where is it taking place?

February 28 - March 1, 2015, in the Santa Clara University building Kennedy Commons near the intersection of Lafayette and Market Street.

What else can we expect at Hack for the Homeless?

Google's Seth Marbin as well as Community Technology Alliance's Jen Padget and Downtown Streets Team's Check Jagoda will be giving talks to kick off the event and remind hackers of the impact they can have in helping their community.

How will you decide between applicants?

SCU students are given priority, and admission to other students are given on a first-come-first-serve basis.

What's the maximum team size?

Teams will have the standard maximum size of four participants.

I've never been to a Hackathon, is that okay?

Not a problem! Hackathons are a perfect time to pick up new skills and see what you can accomplish over a short period. There's lots of beginners at every hackathon, so go be awesome together.

I don't have a team, is that okay?

That's perfectly fine! There are always students looking for teams. We will help link you with other students who are looking for teams before the event starts.
